Ruqi Zhang

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Purdue University. Before that, I was a postdoctoral fellow at Institute for Foundations of Machine Learning, UT Austin. I completed my PhD at Cornell University, where I was advised by Chris De Sa.


I am looking for highly motivated students (PhD, MS, undergrads, and interns) to join our lab. If you are interested, feel free to reach out.

We are hiring Postdocs through IPAI Fellowships and Gilbreth Fellowships. If you're interested in working with me, please get in touch.

Research Interests

I am interested in developing scalable, reliable and efficient probabilistic methods for machine learning and generative AI. Our work bridges theoretical foundations and practical implementations, aiming to address fundamental challenges in building trustworthy and efficient AI systems. Currently, I focus on the following key directions: (1) alignment and safety of foundation models, including LLMs, VLMs, and diffusion models; (2) Probabilistic inference, uncertainty estimation, and Bayesian methods; and (3) Generation and learning on discrete data.
